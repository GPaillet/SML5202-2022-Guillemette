How to use the present

When do you use the "présent de l'indicatif"?

The présent is fairly similar to the English simple present with some differences. You can use présent to talk about a current action, a habitual action or even an action that is about to occur

Example: Je travaille (=I am working), Je travaille le dimanche (=I work on wednesdays), Je travaille demain (= I am working tomorrow)

Reminder: French verbs are divided in three groups, in the first are groups ending with -er (Example: manger, chanter...). In the second, you have the verbs that end with -ir BUT transforms in -issons when conjugated in the first plural pronoun in present (Example: Finir > Nous finissons). In the last group are the other verbs ( Example: Vendre, Coudre, Courir, Craindre...)
