Adjectives in French

What do you take into account when you use an adjective in French?

Adjectives don't work the same in French as it does in English. French adjectives help to determine the gender and the number of the noun(s) they describe. You have to determine if you use the masculine or the feminine form and if you use the singular or the plural form.

Masculine or feminine?

GENDER: masculin ou féminin. To form the feminine form, you have to add an -e to the masculine form

Examples:
Grand --> Grande
Petit --> Petite

WARNING: If the masculine adjective ends in -e, nothing changes.

Particular cases

When adjectives end with an -x, they become -se in the feminine.

Examples:
Courageux --> Courageuse
Curieux --> Curieuse

WARNING: There are some exceptions:
  • Rêveur --> Rêveuse
  • Menteur --> Menteuse
  • Sportif --> Sportive
  • Beau --> Belle
  • Gentil --> Gentille
  • Vieux --> Vieille
  • Blanc --> Blanche

    • Placement in the sentence

    In general, they come after the noun they describe, unlike the English adjectives. There are some exceptions like: haut, petit, vieux, jeune, beau...
