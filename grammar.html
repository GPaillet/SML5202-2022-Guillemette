How to use the present

When do you use the "présent de l'indicatif"?

The présent is fairly similar to the English simple present with some differences. You can use présent to talk about a current action, a habitual action or even an action that is about to occur

Example: Je travaille (=I am working), Je travaille le dimanche (=I work on wednesdays), Je travaille demain (= I am working tomorrow)

Reminder: French verbs are divided in three groups, in the first are groups ending with -er (Example: manger, chanter...). In the second, you have the verbs that end with -ir BUT transforms in -issons when conjugated in the first plural pronoun in present (Example: Finir > Nous finissons). In the last group are the other verbs ( Example: Vendre, Coudre, Courir, Craindre...)

How to use the imperfect

When do you use the "imparfait de l'indicatif"?

There are many situations where you use the imperfect tense, you use it for: recurrent actions , describing feelings, describing age and time, general descriptions in the past or even ongoing actions puncuated by another action

Steps to conjugate a verb in the French imperfect tense

  1. Take the first plural form of the verb in the present tense
  2. Remove -ons ending
  3. Add the appropriate ending

Adjectives in French

What do you take into account when you use an adjective in French?

Adjectives don't work the same in French as it does in English. French adjectives help to determine the gender and the number of the noun(s) they describe. You have to determine if you use the masculine or the feminine form and if you use the singular or the plural form.

Masculine or feminine?

GENDER: masculin ou féminin. To form the feminine form, you have to add an -e to the masculine form

Examples:
Grand --> Grande
Petit --> Petite

WARNING: If the masculine adjective ends in -e, nothing changes.

Particular cases

When adjectives end with an -x, they become -se in the feminine.

Examples:
Courageux --> Courageuse
Curieux --> Curieuse

WARNING: There are some exceptions:
  • Rêveur --> Rêveuse
  • Menteur --> Menteuse
  • Sportif --> Sportive
  • Beau --> Belle
  • Gentil --> Gentille
  • Vieux --> Vieille
  • Blanc --> Blanche

    • Placement in the sentence

    In general, they come after the noun they describe, unlike the English adjectives. There are some exceptions like: haut, petit, vieux, jeune, beau...
