IMPERFECT

How to express a past action in French

B

When do you use the imperfect tense?

The imperfect is one of the two main past tenses in French. It is used to describe continuous past actions but also recurrent actions, feelings, age and time, general descriptions in the past and ongoing actions pertubated by another action

EXAMPLES: J'étais au téléphone quand il est arrivé

B

How to form the imperfect?

Take the "nous" form in present, remove the -ons and add the right ending

Examples:
Nous parlons
Nous parl...
Nous parlions

WARNING: There are some exceptions.

There are very few exceptions for the imperfect

For verbs ending with -cer: You have to add a cedilla (" ç ") in the form. It remains in all the forms except for "nous" and "vous"

For verbs ending with -ger: You have to add a -e for this form. It remains in all the forms except for "nous" and "vous"
